AAM data file format


The BADC holds AAM data in ASCII files. These files are supplied by the European Centre for Medium-range Weather Forecasting (ECMWF) and the Met Office. File names are detailed in the Help file.

Temporal coverage

  • ECMWF data spans 1979 to 1993, with data at 24 hourly intervals.

  • Met Office data are available from 1983 to the present. The Met Office data consists of three subsets:

    1. Data prior to 1st November 1986 in Met Office format (24-hourly intervals).
    2. Data after 1st November 1986 in Met Office format (12-hourly intervals).
    3. Washington-coded data for dates after 1st November 1986 (12-hourly intervals).

Parameters stored

The parameters stored in the ECMWF and most recent (after 1st November 1986) Met Office files are the same, namely (for each time interval stored): 

 
   X1PN, X1PS, X1WN, X1WS
   X2PN, X2PS, X2WN, X2WS
   X3PN, X3PS, X3WN, X3WS

where:

  • X - to be read as chi
  • 1/2/3 represent the AAM component:
    1. component with rotation axis aligned with greenwich meridian;
    2. " " " " " " 90°East;
    3. " " " " " " principal axis of earth's inertia tensor. n.b. X3 has the opposite sign to X3 as defined in BHWW (1983) (5.3) and Hide (1984) (a3) (see references);
  • P/W indicates pressure (also called matter) or wind term. This is calculated using only the part of the atmosphere between 1000 mb and 50 mb.
  • N/S represents Northern or Southern hemisphere.

The early Met Office data contains global wind and pressure terms rather than separate components for each hemisphere and includes an additional "total" term (P+W). The situation can be summarised as follows: 

|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     Dataset           |      Pressure       |      Wind           |  P+W  |
|                       |---------------------|---------------------|       |
|                       | North | South | N+S | North | South | N+S |       |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Met Office pre Nov. 86 |   -   |   -   |  Y  |   -   |   -   |  Y  |   Y   |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Met Office post Nov. 86|   Y   |   Y   |  -  |   Y   |   Y   |  -  |   -   |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|     ECMWF             |   Y   |   Y   |  -  |   Y   |   Y   |  -  |   -   |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|

where "Y" denotes that a term is present and "-" denotes that term is absent (for all three components X1, X2, X3).

In addition, Met Office data post-Nov. 1986 is stored at 12-hourly intervals (earlier data and ECMWF data are 24-hourly).
